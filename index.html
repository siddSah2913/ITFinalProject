What is Mithila?

Mithila is an ancient cultural and geographical region whose living heart lies in Nepal's Madhesh Province - the eastern Terai plains stretching from Saptari in the east to Parsa in the west. Cities like Janakpur, Rajbiraj, Lahan, Jaleshwar, and Birgunj are all part of this cultural world. Mithila culture also extends across the border into northern Bihar, India.

The region is home to the Maithil people, who speak the Maithili language - one of the 22 scheduled languages of India and a major language of Nepal's Madhesh Province. Mithila is celebrated worldwide for its extraordinary tradition of Mithila (Madhubani) painting, still practised today on the walls of homes across Nepal's Terai villages.

Did you know? Mithila is the birthplace of Goddess Sita, the central figure of the Hindu epic Ramayana. Janakpur in Nepal's Madhesh Province is her sacred birthplace and the site of her marriage to Lord Ram - drawing pilgrims from across South Asia and the world.
